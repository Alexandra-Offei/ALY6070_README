# ALY 6070 — Communication & Visualisation for Data Analytics

**Northeastern University · Roux Institute**  
**Course:** ALY 6070 Communication and Visualisation for Data Analytics  
**Language:** R · ggplot2 · R Markdown

---

## Overview

This course focused on translating complex analytical findings into clear, compelling visual narratives for diverse audiences. Projects used R and ggplot2 to explore large real-world datasets, producing publication-quality visualisations and structured analytical reports.

---

## Projects

### Module 1 — Introduction to Data Visualisation
- Foundations of effective visual communication for data analytics
- Chart selection, colour theory, and audience-appropriate design
- R Markdown workflow for reproducible reporting

---

### Module 2 — EDA with R: New Orleans Police Stops
**Dataset:** 512,092 traffic stop records · New Orleans, Louisiana · as of April 2020 · 32 variables

- Comprehensive exploratory data analysis on half a million police interactions
- Traffic stop frequency patterns by **hour of day** — identifying peak enforcement periods
- **Age distribution by race** across all traffic stops — horizontal boxplot comparison
- Bar plots and scatter plots revealing enforcement patterns across demographics
- Data cleaning: time column parsing, factor encoding, missing value handling

**Key Visualisations:**
- Histogram of traffic stops by hour (24-hour cycle)
- Boxplot: subject age by subject race (horizontal orientation)
- Bar plots: stop frequency by race and outcome category
- Scatter plots: relationships between stop variables

**Tools:** R · ggplot2 · dplyr · hms · R Markdown

### Module 4 — Air Quality Project Proposal

- Dataset overview and initial exploratory visualisation

### Final Project — Air Quality Trends & Geographic Disparities ⭐
**Dataset:** EPA air quality data · 18,025 observations · multiple U.S. regions

**Title:** Analysing Air Quality Trends and Geographic Disparities in Pollution Levels

- Full analytical report on U.S. air pollution trends from 2005 to 2020
- **Trend analysis:** Sharp decline in pollution levels post-2005; post-2020 stabilisation linked to pandemic-related emission reductions
- **Geographic disparities:** Regional variation in Ozone, NO₂, SO₂, and fine particulate concentrations
- **Public health framing:** Linking pollutant exposure to respiratory and cardiovascular outcomes
- Evidence-based insights for environmental policy and infrastructure planning

**Tools:** R · ggplot2 · dplyr · tidyr · R Markdown · PDF output via XeLaTeX
